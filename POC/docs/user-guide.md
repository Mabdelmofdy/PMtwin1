# User Guide

This guide explains how to use the PMTwin platform as a professional, company, or administrator.

## Roles

- **Professional** — Individual who can create opportunities (needs/offers), apply to opportunities, and participate in deals.
- **Consultant** — Professional with verified expertise; same capabilities as professional with higher visibility in matching.
- **Company (company_owner)** — Organization that can create opportunities, invite or accept applicants, and participate in consortium or multi-party deals.
- **Admin** — Platform administrator with access to vetting, matching reports, and system settings.

## Creating an Opportunity

1. Go to **Opportunities** → **Create** (or Collaboration Wizard).
2. Choose **intent**: Request (need) or Offer.
3. Fill in: title, description, industry, location, timeline (start/end), exchange mode (cash, barter, equity, etc.), budget or value range, required skills, scope of work, and milestones.
4. Submit; the opportunity is published and becomes available for matching and applications.

## Applying to an Opportunity

1. Find an opportunity (browse or via match recommendations).
2. Click **Apply** and submit a proposal (rate, duration, approach).
3. Your application is visible to the opportunity owner. Statuses: pending, reviewing, shortlisted, in_negotiation, accepted, rejected, withdrawn.

## Matching

- The engine suggests **matches** between your needs/offers and others’ offers/needs.
- Match types: **One-way** (your need + their offer, or vice versa), **Two-way** (barter), **Consortium** (one lead need + multiple member offers), **Circular** (chain of value exchange).
- You can **accept** or **decline** a match. Accepted matches can proceed to negotiation and deal.

## Negotiation

- For an accepted match or accepted application, a **negotiation** session is created.
- Parties exchange **proposals** (value, timeline, payment schedule) in **rounds**.
- Statuses: open, counter_offered, agreed, failed, expired.
- When status is **agreed**, the system can create a **deal**.

## Deals

- A **deal** represents the agreed collaboration: participants, title, opportunity reference, timeline, exchange terms, milestones, and optional contract reference.
- Deal statuses: negotiating, draft, review, signing, active, execution, delivery, completed, closed.
- You can view deals in **Deals**, approve drafts, sign, and track milestones.

## Contracts

- When a deal reaches **signing**, a **contract** is created with parties, scope, payment mode, duration, agreed value, and (optionally) a milestones snapshot.
- All parties must sign. Contract status: pending, active, completed, terminated.
- Execution (milestones, deliverables) is tracked on the **deal**; the contract is the legal record.

## Consortium and Circular Exchange

- **Consortium**: One party (lead) has a need that requires multiple roles (e.g. investor, contractor, supplier). The lead forms a consortium; members join via role slots. Participant drop-out can trigger **replacement** flow (invite new candidate for the same role).
- **Circular**: Three or more parties form a cycle (A provides to B, B to C, C to A). Value circulates without a single payer.
